Maximum likelihood in normal linear regression

Hold the observed sample fixed, then move the candidate parameters. Compare the probability-model view of maximum likelihood with the geometric least-squares view in which the same regression coefficients minimize the sum of squared residuals.

At each displayed predictor value, the sideways curve is the conditional density of the outcome. Its center lies on the regression line.

The vertical lines are residuals, eᵢ = yᵢ − ŷᵢ. Their shaded square areas represent eᵢ². Ordinary least squares chooses β₀ and β₁ to make the total area, SSE = Σeᵢ², as small as possible.

Two views of the same coefficient problem

In the probability view, β₀ and β₁ shift the conditional normal distributions to maximize the joint density of the observed sample. In the least-squares view, the same coefficients move the line to minimize Σeᵢ². Under normal homoskedastic errors, both criteria produce the same coefficient estimates.
